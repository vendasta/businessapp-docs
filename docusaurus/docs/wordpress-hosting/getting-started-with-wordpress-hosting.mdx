# Getting Started with WordPress Hosting

WordPress Hosting helps you build and run fast, secure websites with built-in eCommerce, visual editing, and managed hosting. This guide walks you through choosing a template or importing a site, customizing your design, setting up your store, connecting your domain, and tracking performance.

## Getting started checklist

1. [Choose a template or import your site](#step-1-choose-a-template-or-import-your-site): Start from a pre-built layout or bring an existing WordPress site
2. [Customize your site](#step-2-customize-your-site): Add your logo, colors, and content with Divi Builder
3. [Set up your store (if selling online)](#step-3-set-up-your-store-if-selling-online): Configure WooCommerce for products, payments, and shipping
4. [Connect your domain (Pro only)](#step-4-connect-your-domain-pro-only): Point your custom domain to your site
5. [Connect analytics](#step-5-connect-analytics): Track visitors and performance with Google Analytics

## Step 1: Choose a template or import your site

New sites start from a template or from an existing WordPress export. Templates include themes, plugins, and page layouts so you can launch quickly.

### How to create a new site from a template

1. Log in to Business App and open **WordPress Hosting**.
2. Create a new site and choose one of the seven pre-built templates (Retail, Services, Education, B2B, Home Services, Art/Photography, or Health/Fitness).
3. Enter your business name and tagline when prompted.
4. The template installs Divi Builder and WooCommerce automatically.

See the [Templates overview](./templates/index.mdx) for custom templates and hiding pre-built options.

### How to import an existing WordPress site

1. Create your WordPress Hosting account and a new site.
2. Install the **All-in-One WP Migration** plugin on both your original site and the new WordPress Hosting site.
3. On your original site: go to **All-in-One WP Migration** > **Export**, then download the export file.
4. On your new site: go to **All-in-One WP Migration** > **Import**, then upload the file.

See the [Import guide](./import/index.md) for migration options, file size limits, and manual import steps.

:::info
WordPress Hosting Standard uses subdomains only. Custom domains require **WordPress Hosting Pro**.
:::

## Step 2: Customize your site

Use Divi Builder to edit your site visually without coding. Add your logo, change theme colors, and update pages and images.

### How to customize with Divi Builder

1. Go to **WordPress Hosting** and open your site's **WordPress Dashboard**.
2. Click **Customize your site** or go to your homepage with Divi Builder enabled.
3. Edit text and images directly on the page. Use **Divi** > **General Settings** to add your logo and **Divi** > **Theme Customizer** to set theme colors.
4. Add or edit pages from the **Pages** tab in WordPress.

See [Edit your site with Divi Builder](./wordpress-dashboard/plugins/divi-builder/edit-your-site-with-divi-builder.md) for a full walkthrough.

:::tip
The **Getting Started Card** on your dashboard Overview page provides quick links to customize your homepage, add your logo, set theme colors, and add pages.
:::

## Step 3: Set up your store (if selling online)

If you use an eCommerce template, configure WooCommerce to add products, set up payments, and define shipping.

### How to set up WooCommerce

1. Go to **WordPress Hosting** > **WordPress Dashboard**.
2. Open **WooCommerce** > **Settings** and click **Setup Wizard** (under the **Help** tab) to run the configuration.
3. Add products under **Products** > **Add New**.
4. Configure payment methods under **WooCommerce** > **Settings** > **Payments**.
5. Set up shipping under **WooCommerce** > **Settings** > **Shipping**.

See [Set up your WooCommerce store](./wordpress-dashboard/plugins/woocommerce/setup-your-woocommerce-store.md) and [WooCommerce Payments](./wordpress-dashboard/plugins/woocommerce/set-up-woocommerce-payments.md) for details.

## Step 4: Connect your domain (Pro only)

WordPress Hosting Pro supports custom domains. Configure DNS at your registrar and connect the domain in WordPress Hosting.

### How to connect a custom domain

1. At your domain registrar, add an **A Record** for your root domain pointing to `34.149.86.124`.
2. Add a **CNAME Record** for `www` pointing to `host.websiteprohosting.com`.
3. Go to **WordPress Hosting** > **Domains**.
4. Click **Connect a Domain**, enter your domain, and click **Add Domain**.
5. Wait for the domain to show as **Connected**. WordPress Hosting provisions SSL automatically.
6. Use **Make Primary** to set your main domain if you have multiple connected domains.

See [Domain setup](./domains/connect-a-custom-domain.md) and the [Domains overview](./domains/index.mdx) for DNS details and troubleshooting.

:::note
DNS propagation can take from a few minutes to 48 hours. SSL certificates are issued automatically by Let's Encrypt.
:::

## Step 5: Connect analytics

Track visitors, page views, and traffic sources. Connect your own Google Analytics account for accurate, site-specific data.

### How to connect Google Analytics

1. Go to **Business App** > **Administration** > **Connections** to connect Google Analytics, or
2. In **WordPress Dashboard** > **Settings** > **General**, enter your Tracking ID in **Custom Google Analytics Tracking ID**.

See the [Analytics overview](./analytics/index.mdx) and [Add your own analytics account](./analytics/add-your-own-analytics-account.md) for setup and data options.

:::tip
Connecting your own Google Analytics account gives you more accurate data than the default shared view.
:::

## Frequently asked questions

<details>
<summary>What is the difference between WordPress Hosting Standard and Pro?</summary>

Standard includes templates, Divi Builder, WooCommerce, daily backups (2 retained), and subdomain-only hosting. Pro adds custom domains, staging environments, extended backup retention (up to 90 days), phpMyAdmin, SFTP, and no plugin restrictions.
</details>

<details>
<summary>Do I need to buy SSL separately?</summary>

No. WordPress Hosting automatically provisions free SSL certificates from Let's Encrypt for all connected domains.
</details>


<details>
<summary>Can I use a custom domain with Standard?</summary>

No. Custom domains are only available with WordPress Hosting Pro. Standard sites use subdomains (e.g., mybusiness.websitepro.hosting).
</details>
