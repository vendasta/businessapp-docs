## What is the Payments page?

The **Payments** page shows the status of the payment account you use to collect payments from your customers. What you see on this page updates automatically based on whether you've connected an account and its current status.

## Why does the Payments page status matter?

- **Know your setup status at a glance**: A badge confirms when your account is fully connected and ready to accept payments.
- **Avoid interruptions**: Alerts let you know about outstanding requirements before they affect your ability to receive payouts.
- **Understand issues quickly**: A banner explains when your account has been rejected so you can take corrective action.

## What you'll see on the Payments page

- **Informational card**: Shown if you haven't connected a payment account yet. It explains how to get started collecting payments.
- **Connected badge**: Shown once your account is fully connected and ready to accept payments.
- **Status alert**: Shown when your connected account has outstanding requirements, is temporarily disabled, or has upcoming requirements due.
- **Account-rejected banner**: Shown if your connected account has been rejected.

## Frequently Asked Questions

<details>
<summary>Why do I still see the informational card about payments?</summary>

The informational card only appears if you haven't connected a payment account yet. Once you connect one, the card is replaced with your account's current status.
</details>

<details>
<summary>What does the Connected badge mean?</summary>

The Connected badge confirms your payment account is fully set up and ready to accept payments from your customers.
</details>

<details>
<summary>What should I do if I see a status alert on the Payments page?</summary>

Follow the instructions in the alert to resolve outstanding requirements. Leaving them unresolved can pause your ability to receive payouts.
</details>

<details>
<summary>What does an account-rejected banner mean?</summary>

It means your connected payment account did not pass verification. Review the banner for details on what to correct.
</details>
