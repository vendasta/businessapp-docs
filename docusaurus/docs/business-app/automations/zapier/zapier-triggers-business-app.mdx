When an event happens in another app — like a new payment in QuickBooks or a new customer in your booking system — Zapier can fire one of your Business App automations automatically. This page walks you through setting that up.

## Use case example

**New QuickBooks payment → send a review request**

When a customer pays an invoice in QuickBooks Online, Zapier fires an automation in Business App that sends the customer a review request. No manual follow-up needed.

## Prerequisites

- An active [Zapier](https://zapier.com/) account
- A Business App automation using the **Triggered via Zapier** trigger (see Step 6 below)
- The automation must be turned on

## How to set up

### Step 1: Select the trigger

In Zapier, create a new Zap and choose the app and event that will start your workflow. For example, select **QuickBooks Online** as the app and **New Payment** as the trigger event.

You can use any trigger available in Zapier — the steps that follow are the same regardless of which trigger app you choose.

### Step 2: Choose Business App as the action app

After setting up the trigger, search for and select **Business App** as the action app.

<img src={require('./img/zapier_app.png').default} alt="Business App selected as the action app in Zapier" width="60%" />

### Step 3: Choose the action

Select **Run Automation** as the action. This tells Zapier to fire one of your Business App automations when the trigger event occurs.

You can change the action at any time by clicking the dropdown and selecting a different option. Click **Continue** when ready.

<img src={require('./img/zapier_action.png').default} alt="Run Automation selected as the action in Zapier" width="60%" />

### Step 4: Sign in to Business App

Click **Sign in** to connect your Business App account. You'll be redirected to a login page where you'll enter your Account ID and grant the necessary permissions. After that, you'll be returned to Zapier automatically.

You only need to do this once. Future Zaps using Business App will use the same connected account.

<img src={require('./img/zapier_signin.png').default} alt="Sign in prompt for connecting Business App to Zapier" width="60%" />

<img src={require('./img/zapier_allow_access.png').default} alt="Allow access screen for Business App permissions in Zapier" width="60%" />

### Step 5: Enter the Organization ID

The Organization ID field is required. It is automatically populated based on the Account ID you used when signing in.

<img src={require('./img/zapier_organization_id.png').default} alt="Organization ID field auto-populated in Zapier" width="60%" />

### Step 6: Set the trigger type

In the **Trigger** field, select **Triggered via Zapier**. This must match the trigger your automation uses in Business App.

### Step 7: Enter the Automation ID

In the **Automation ID** field, enter the ID of the automation you want Zapier to run. This field is a searchable dropdown — you can search by automation name or ID.

<img src={require('./img/zapier_automation_id.png').default} alt="Automation ID field in Zapier" width="60%" />

The automation must be turned on for it to run when triggered from Zapier.

### Step 8: Test the step

Before publishing the Zap, use Zapier's test feature to confirm everything is working. This can catch missing fields or incorrect data before the Zap goes live.

<img src={require('./img/zapier_testing.png').default} alt="Testing the Zapier step before publishing" width="60%" />

A **Run Automation Successful** message confirms the Zap is ready to publish.

## Setting up the automation in Business App

Your automation must use the **Triggered via Zapier** trigger to be fireable from Zapier. To set this up:

1. Go to **Business App** > **Automations**
2. Create a new automation or open an existing one
3. Set the trigger to **Triggered via Zapier** (found under **Advanced** in the trigger picker)
4. Add the steps you want to run (for example, **Send a review request** to a contact)
5. Turn the automation on using the toggle at the top right

## Frequently Asked Questions

<details>
<summary>What trigger does my automation need to use?</summary>

Your automation must use the **Triggered via Zapier** trigger, found under the Advanced section of the trigger picker in Business App. Automations using other triggers cannot be fired from Zapier.
</details>

<details>
<summary>Can I use any trigger app in Zapier?</summary>

Yes. The trigger that fires your Business App automation can come from any app available in Zapier — QuickBooks, HubSpot, Google Sheets, Calendly, and thousands more. The Business App action steps are the same regardless of the trigger app.
</details>

<details>
<summary>What happens if the automation is turned off?</summary>

If the automation is off, incoming Zapier triggers will be ignored. The automation must be active for it to run.
</details>

<details>
<summary>Can I pass data from the trigger app into my automation?</summary>

Yes. When Zapier fires the automation, it can include data from the trigger event. How that data is used depends on how your automation steps are configured. Check the Zapier documentation for details on mapping fields between steps.
</details>

<details>
<summary>Do I need to sign in to Business App every time I create a new Zap?</summary>

No. Once you connect your Business App account in Zapier, it stays connected. You can reuse the same connection across all your Zaps.
</details>

<details>
<summary>How do I find my Automation ID?</summary>

Use the searchable dropdown in Zapier's Automation ID field — you can type the name of your automation and select it from the results. You don't need to look up the ID manually.
</details>
