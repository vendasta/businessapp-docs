When an event happens in another app, like a new payment in QuickBooks or a new customer in your booking system, Zapier can fire one of your Business App automations automatically. This page walks you through setting that up.

## Prerequisites

- An active [Zapier](https://zapier.com/) account
- An account with the external app you want to use as your trigger (for example, QuickBooks Online, Google Forms, or any other Zapier-supported app)
- A Business App automation using the **Triggered via Zapier** trigger
- The automation must be turned on

## Examples

**Google Form event registration → send a confirmation email**

When someone fills out your event registration form in Google Forms and provides their company name, Zapier passes that data to a Business App automation. The automation looks up a contact at that company and sends them a confirmation email letting them know their company is registered for the event.

## How to set up

There are three parts: setting up the automation in Business App first, then configuring the Zap in Zapier, then testing and publishing.

The steps below use Google Forms as the example trigger app. Your external app (Google Form, QuickBooks, etc.) should already be set up and collecting data before you begin.

### Part 1: Set up the automation in Business App

Your automation must use the `Triggered via Zapier` trigger so Zapier knows which automation to fire.

**Step 1:** Go to **Business App** > **Automations** and create a new automation or open an existing one.

**Step 2:** Set the trigger to `Triggered via Zapier`, found under `Advanced` in the trigger picker.

<div style={{ textAlign: 'center', marginBottom: '1.5rem' }}>
  <img src={require('./img/businessApp-triggered-via-zap.png').default} alt="Triggered via Zapier option under the Advanced section in the Business App trigger picker" width="75%" />
</div>

**Step 3:** In the trigger's side panel, note your **Account ID** and **Automation ID**. You'll need both when setting up the Zap in Zapier.

<div style={{ textAlign: 'center', marginBottom: '1.5rem' }}>
  <img src={require('./img/businessApp-AutomationID.png').default} alt="Edit trigger side panel in Business App showing the Account ID and Automation ID fields" width="75%" />
</div>

**Step 4:** Define your **Payload data**. This is the data your external app will send to Business App when the automation runs. For each piece of data, enter a **Key name** (a label you choose — make it descriptive, like `company-name`) and select its **Type**.

The Type should reflect how that data is used in Business App. In this example, the Google Form collects a company name, and in Business App a company is identified by its **Company Id** — so set the Type to `Company Id`.

If your automation needs more than one piece of data from the external app, click <img src={require('./img/businessApp-add-data-value.png').default} alt="Add data value button" style={{ verticalAlign: 'middle', display: 'inline', width: '15%' }} /> to add additional fields.

<div style={{ textAlign: 'center', marginBottom: '1.5rem' }}>
  <img src={require('./img/BusinessApp-payload data.png').default} alt="Payload data section in the Business App trigger editor showing a company-name key with Company Id selected as the type" width="75%" />
</div>

**Step 5:** Add the steps you want the automation to run. Because the payload only provides a company name, you can't send an email directly — you first need to identify who to email. Add a `Get contact from company` step to retrieve a contact at that company, then add a `Send a plain text email via Conversations` step to send them the confirmation.

<div style={{ textAlign: 'center', marginBottom: '1.5rem' }}>
  <img src={require('./img/businessApp-steps-sfter-zapier-trigger.png').default} alt="Business App automation showing a Get contact from company step followed by a Send a plain text email via Conversations step" width="60%" />
</div>

**Step 6:** Turn the automation on using the toggle at the top right.

### Part 2: Configure the Zap in Zapier

**Step 7:** In Zapier, create a new Zap and choose the app and event that will start your workflow. For example, select **Google Forms** as the app and `New Form Response` as the trigger event.

<div style={{ textAlign: 'center', marginBottom: '1.5rem' }}>
  <img src={require('./img/zapier-google form.png').default} alt="Zapier Zap editor showing Google Forms selected as the trigger app with New Form Response as the trigger event" width="75%" />
</div>

You can use any trigger available in Zapier: the steps that follow are the same regardless of which trigger app you choose.

**Step 8:** After setting up the trigger, search for and select **Business App** as the action app.

<div style={{ textAlign: 'center', marginBottom: '1.5rem' }}>
  <img src={require('./img/zapier-external-to-businessApp-automation.png').default} alt="Zapier Zap editor showing Google Forms as the trigger and Business App as the action step" width="50%" />
</div>

**Step 9:** Select `Run Automation` as the action. This tells Zapier to fire one of your Business App automations when the trigger event occurs. Click `Continue` when ready.

<div style={{ textAlign: 'center', marginBottom: '1.5rem' }}>
  <img src={require('./img/zapier-run-automation-event.png').default} alt="Zapier action setup showing Business App selected with Run Automation as the action event" width="75%" />
</div>

**Step 10:** In the `Account` field, click `Select` to connect your Business App account. You'll be redirected to a login page where you'll enter your Account ID and grant the necessary permissions. After that, you'll be returned to Zapier automatically.

You only need to do this once. Future Zaps using Business App will use the same connected account.

<div style={{ textAlign: 'center', marginBottom: '1.5rem' }}>
  <img src={require('./img/zapier_allow_access.png').default} alt="Allow access screen for Business App permissions in Zapier" width="60%" />
</div>

**Step 11:** In the `Automation ID` field, search for and select the automation you set up in Part 1. The dropdown lists your available automations by name.

<div style={{ textAlign: 'center', marginBottom: '1.5rem' }}>
  <img src={require('./img/zapier-set-businessApp-automation-id.png').default} alt="Zapier Automation ID field showing a searchable list of Business App automations to select from" width="75%" />
</div>

**Step 12:** Once the automation is selected, the payload data fields you defined in Part 1 appear below. Map each field to the corresponding value from your trigger app — in this example, map `company-name` to the Google Form question that collects the company name. This tells Zapier exactly which piece of incoming data to pass to Business App.

<div style={{ textAlign: 'center', marginBottom: '1.5rem' }}>
  <img src={require('./img/zapier_sending-key-values.png').default} alt="Zapier action configuration showing the company-name payload field mapped to the Google Form question asking what company the respondent is with" width="75%" />
</div>

### Part 3: Test and publish

**Step 13:** Before publishing the Zap, use Zapier's test feature with a sample Google Form response to confirm everything is working. A **Run Automation Successful** message confirms the automation ran correctly in Business App.

<div style={{ textAlign: 'center', marginBottom: '1.5rem' }}>
  <img src={require('./img/zapier_run-automation-successful.png').default} alt="Zapier test page showing a Run Automation Successful message confirming the Business App automation ran correctly" width="75%" />
</div>

A `Run Automation Successful` message confirms the Zap is ready to publish.

**Step 13:** Name your Zap and publish it.

## Frequently Asked Questions

<details>
<summary>What trigger does my automation need to use?</summary>

Your automation must use the `Triggered via Zapier` trigger, found under the `Advanced` section of the trigger picker in Business App. Automations using other triggers cannot be fired from Zapier.
</details>

<details>
<summary>Can I use any trigger app in Zapier?</summary>

Yes. The trigger that fires your Business App automation can come from any app available in Zapier: QuickBooks, HubSpot, Google Sheets, Calendly, and thousands more. The Business App action steps are the same regardless of the trigger app.
</details>

<details>
<summary>What happens if the automation is turned off?</summary>

If the automation is off, incoming Zapier triggers will be ignored. The automation must be active for it to run.
</details>

<details>
<summary>Can I pass data from the trigger app into my automation?</summary>

Yes. When Zapier fires the automation, it can include data from the trigger event. How that data is used depends on how your automation steps are configured. Check the Zapier documentation for details on mapping fields between steps.
</details>

<details>
<summary>Do I need to sign in to Business App every time I create a new Zap?</summary>

No. Once you connect your Business App account in Zapier, it stays connected. You can reuse the same connection across all your Zaps.
</details>

<details>
<summary>How do I find my Automation ID?</summary>

Use the searchable dropdown in Zapier's `Automation ID` field: you can type the name of your automation and select it from the results. You don't need to look up the ID manually.
</details>
