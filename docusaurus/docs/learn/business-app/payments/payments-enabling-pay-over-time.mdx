Once a business is ready to offer Pay Over Time to their customers, enabling it involves a few straightforward steps — but there are important prerequisites and timelines to understand. This lesson walks through the full setup process, what to expect during Wisetack's approval review, and how to answer the questions that come up most often.

:::tip
Stripe Payments must be fully set up before Pay Over Time can be enabled. If Payments isn't connected yet, start there first.
:::

## <div className="principle-area"><span className="principle-area__icon">🔧</span>Setting Up Pay Over Time</div>

> **Prerequisite** Stripe (Payments) must be connected before Pay Over Time can be enabled. The setup option will not appear otherwise.

> **Initiation** Once Payments is connected, navigate to the Pay Over Time setup section in the app. Starting the setup kicks off the Wisetack application on the business's behalf.

> **Already submitted?** If a business attempts to re-apply while their application is still pending, they will be directed to a confirmation screen indicating the application has already been submitted.

> **If declined** Wisetack sends a decline email outlining the criteria. If the business later meets the criteria and wants to re-apply, contact support to obtain a new application link.

<div className="in-practice">

**In Practice:** Walk through setup with the business in real time when possible. It's quick, and completing it together ensures nothing gets missed — especially making sure Stripe is connected first.

</div>

---

## <div className="principle-area"><span className="principle-area__icon">⏳</span>Approval Process</div>

> **Review timeline** Wisetack reviews applications within up to 5 business days, though most decisions come through in 2–3 business days.

> **Criteria** Wisetack evaluates annual revenue ($200K+), time in business (1+ year), and customer experience data including online reviews.

> **If approved** The business receives an email confirmation from Wisetack. Pay Over Time automatically becomes available on all invoices over $500 — no additional activation needed.

> **If declined** Wisetack sends an email explaining the criteria. Once the business meets the requirements, a new application link is needed — this is handled through support.

<div className="in-practice">

**In Practice:** Set the right expectation upfront: approval is not instant. Let the business know to expect a response within 2–3 business days, and suggest they enable Payments in the meantime if they haven't already.

</div>

---

## <div className="principle-area"><span className="principle-area__icon">❓</span>Common Questions</div>

> **Why confirm job completion before releasing payment?** About 10% of end-customers are declined by Wisetack. If the job is already done and the customer is declined, the business can still collect payment — but only through Stripe (not Pay Over Time). For high-ticket jobs, it's best practice to send the invoice before work begins so the customer can apply and get approved in advance.

> **What happens in a dispute?** If a dispute arises, Wisetack contacts the business directly, verifies the job details and amounts with both parties, then pulls back the funds and adjusts the customer's loan balance accordingly. The business doesn't need to manage this process — Wisetack handles it.

<div className="in-practice">

**In Practice:** The "why confirm before releasing?" question comes up frequently from businesses with high-ticket services. The practical advice is simple: send the invoice early. That way, the customer is pre-approved and the business knows the funds are secured before the work begins.

</div>
