Payments is one of the most impactful features you can introduce to a business. It enables mobile and online payment collection, gets businesses paid faster, and integrates directly into the inbox workflow their team already uses. This lesson covers how it works, how to set it up, and how to handle pushback.

:::tip
Payments works best for businesses that already use the inbox regularly — the payment request flow is built right into the conversation view, so adoption tends to feel natural.
:::

## <div className="principle-area"><span className="principle-area__icon">💳</span>How Payments Works</div>

> **Payment requests** are sent directly from the conversation view in Inbox — the same place the business is already communicating with customers.

> **Payment processor** Payments is powered by Stripe, a trusted and widely used payment platform.

> **Key benefits** include mobile payments, same-day payouts, convenient online payment links, and time savings for the business.

<div className="in-practice">

**In Practice:** When walking a business through Payments, open the inbox and show how a payment request appears in the conversation thread. Seeing it in context — rather than as a standalone feature — makes the workflow click immediately.

</div>

---

## <div className="principle-area"><span className="principle-area__icon">🏦</span>Setting Up Payments</div>

> **Setup path** To connect Payments, navigate to Payments > Payments Setup > Connect a bank account. The business will follow the Stripe onboarding flow.

> **What's enabled** Once connected, the business can send payment requests from any customer conversation in Inbox. Customers receive a link and can pay online.

<div className="in-practice">

**In Practice:** If a business hesitates during setup, reassure them that Stripe is one of the most trusted payment processors in the world. Millions of businesses use it for exactly this kind of mobile and online payment workflow.

</div>

---

## <div className="principle-area"><span className="principle-area__icon">🛡️</span>Common Objections</div>

> **"I'm happy with my current solution"** Ask about their current processing fee. Does their solution support Apple Pay or Google Pay? Can their team collect payments by phone? These questions often reveal gaps Payments can fill.

> **"People pay in the shop"** Ask how often they need to collect payment over the phone, or for jobs completed at a customer's home. Payments handles those situations easily.

> **"I get a better rate with my existing processor"** Payments doesn't have to replace their current processor. Even as a backup option, the convenience — especially for Google/Apple Pay or remote payments — can be worth a slightly higher fee.

<div className="in-practice">

**In Practice:** The goal isn't always a full switch. Framing Payments as an additional tool — one that covers scenarios their current processor can't — lowers resistance and opens the door to adoption.

</div>
