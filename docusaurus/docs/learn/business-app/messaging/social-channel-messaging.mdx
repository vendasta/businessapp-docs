<div className="principle-area"><span className="principle-area__icon">🌐</span>Google Messaging</div>

Connecting your Google account to the app automatically enables Google Messaging for your Google My Business (GMB) listing. Once connected, inbound Google messages arrive in your Inbox as leads — no separate app required.

### How It Appears in the App

> When a potential customer messages your business through Google, their inquiry appears in the app as a lead with the source listed as **Added from Google**. You can reply without leaving the app, and the full conversation history is preserved.

### Google Lead Contact Info

> By default, Google leads do not have a phone number or email associated with them. Collect this information directly in the conversation and update the contact record so you can reach them via text or email going forward.

### Google Local Services Ads (GLSA)

> If your business uses Google Local Services Ads, Google Messaging is also enabled on your GLSA listing. Normal GLSA leads cost approximately $25 each — but leads that come in through the messenger are charged at roughly half that rate.

### Red Notification Warning

> Any account that is not connected to the Google API (which also enables Google Messaging) will display a red notification. This is the prompt to complete the connection.

<div className="principle-area"><span className="principle-area__icon">📸</span>Instagram Messaging</div>

Instagram Messaging works with Professional (Business) accounts that are connected to a Facebook Business Page. Because Facebook owns Instagram, all authentication for the connection flows through Facebook.

### How It Appears in the App

> Once connected, Instagram direct messages appear in the app as a lead with the source listed as **Instagram**. Replies sent from the app are reflected in the business's Instagram account — the connection is fully bidirectional.

### Instagram Lead Contact Info

> Instagram leads do not include a phone number or email by default. Ask for this information in the conversation and update the contact record to enable direct text or email follow-up.

### Deleted and Unsent Messages

> If a message is deleted (unsent) on Instagram, it is also removed from the conversation in the app. You cannot delete messages from within the app — to unsend a message, go to Instagram directly.

### Connection Requirements

> The Instagram account must be a Business account (a type of Professional account) and must be connected to a Facebook Business Page. Personal Instagram accounts cannot be connected — this is a limitation of the API, not a product choice.

<div className="principle-area"><span className="principle-area__icon">👍</span>Facebook Messaging</div>

Facebook Messaging for Business Pages lets customers reach out via the "Send Message" button on a Facebook Business Page. The app mirrors the Facebook messaging experience, so conversations are visible and manageable in both places simultaneously.

### How It Appears in the App

> When a customer messages your Facebook Business Page, their inquiry appears in the app as a lead with the source listed as **Added from Facebook**. You can reply from the app, and the conversation history is preserved in both the app and Facebook.

### Facebook Lead Contact Info

> Facebook leads do not include a phone number or email by default. Collect this information from the customer and update their contact record to enable direct outreach via text or email.

### Enabling Facebook Messaging

> Connecting your Facebook Business Page to the app does **not** automatically enable Facebook Messaging. Messaging for a Business Page must be turned on in Facebook's own settings. All messaging configurations — including away messages and instant replies — remain in Facebook.

### Message History Is Mirrored

> Because the app mirrors Facebook messaging, users can reply from the app, from Facebook, or from both. The full conversation history stays in sync across both platforms.

<div className="principle-area"><span className="principle-area__icon">🔌</span>Connecting Social Channels</div>

All social channel connections are found in the same location: **Settings &gt; Online Profiles**. Each platform has its own authentication path.

### Connecting Google

> Navigate to **Settings &gt; Online Profiles &gt; Google**, then select **Sign in to Google My Business** or **Reconnect Google**. Sign in with the admin credentials for your Google My Business account and approve the connection prompts. Connecting Google automatically enables Google Messaging for the GMB listing.

### Connecting Instagram

> Navigate to **Settings &gt; Online Profiles &gt; Instagram** and select **Connect a professional account**. This opens a Facebook login modal — sign in with the Facebook account that manages the connected Instagram account. Then: select the Instagram accounts to connect, select the linked Facebook Business Page, grant all required permissions, confirm, and finalize the connection back in the app.

### Connecting Facebook

> Navigate to **Settings &gt; Online Profiles &gt; Facebook** and sign in with the Facebook admin profile. After connecting, go to Facebook settings to enable messaging for the Business Page if it is not already active.

### Key Differences at a Glance

> **Google**: connecting automatically enables messaging. **Facebook**: connecting does NOT automatically enable messaging — that must be done in Facebook. **Instagram**: authentication flows through Facebook; a Business account connected to a Facebook Business Page is required.
