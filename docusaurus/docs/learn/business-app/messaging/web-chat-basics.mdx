:::tip

:::

## <div className="principle-area"><span className="principle-area__icon">💬</span>How Web Chat Works</div>

### The visitor experience

> When a visitor arrives on your website and types a message into the Web Chat widget, the app automatically takes over — no one needs to be monitoring in real time.

### Automatic qualification flow

> The app sends an auto-response, runs a spam check, collects the visitor's name, phone number, and email, and confirms TCPA text consent before anything reaches your team.

### Leads pushed to Inbox

> Once a visitor completes the flow, the conversation is pushed into your Inbox as a lead. From there, you can respond and continue the conversation via text or email.

### Why response speed matters

> Responding quickly to new leads significantly increases your chance of converting a website visitor into a paying customer. The faster you reply, the better.

### Installing the widget

> To install Web Chat, add the provided code snippet to the header or footer of your website. Installation details are available at Settings > Web Chat.

<div className="in-practice">
**In practice:** A potential customer visits your website at 9 PM and types "Do you offer same-day service?" Web Chat collects their info, confirms consent, and places the conversation in your Inbox. You reply the next morning — and the lead is still warm because the app already handled the initial touchpoint.
</div>

## <div className="principle-area"><span className="principle-area__icon">⚙️</span>Web Chat Settings</div>

### Enabling and disabling

> Go to Settings > Web Chat to turn the widget on or off at any time without removing it from your website.

### Avatar, color, and display name

> Customize how your widget looks and what name appears in the chat window to match your brand.

### Business hours

> Enable the Business Hours setting to automatically show visitors a message like "We're closed — expect a response when we reopen" during off hours.

### Zip code collection

> Optionally require visitors to enter their zip code during the chat flow — useful for businesses with geographic service areas.

### Messaging customizations

> Set your greeting message, follow-up message, and after-hours follow-up message to match your tone and workflow.

<div className="in-practice">
**In practice:** A landscaping company sets their business hours in Web Chat so weekend visitors see a clear message about when to expect a reply. They also customize the greeting to say "Hi! What outdoor project can we help you with?" — making the first interaction feel personal before anyone on the team responds.
</div>

## <div className="principle-area"><span className="principle-area__icon">📍</span>Multi-Location Web Chat</div>

### How routing works

> When Multi-Location Web Chat is enabled, the widget asks the visitor which location they want after their contact info is collected. The lead is then routed to that specific location in the app.

### Location limit

> Multi-Location Web Chat supports up to 4 locations on a single website widget.

### Why this matters

> Without location routing, multi-location businesses may receive leads at the wrong location or lose context about which team should respond. Routing keeps leads organized from the start.

<div className="in-practice">
**In practice:** A dental group with three offices uses Multi-Location Web Chat so that leads from their shared website automatically route to the correct office. Each location's team only sees leads intended for them — no confusion, no missed inquiries.
</div>

## <div className="principle-area"><span className="principle-area__icon">🛡️</span>Common Objections</div>

### "I don't have time for live chat"

> Web Chat is not live chat. The app handles the initial auto-response, qualification, and contact collection automatically. Your team only steps in once the lead is already in the Inbox.

### "I already get enough business"

> Web Chat doesn't just generate more leads — it streamlines operations. It can reduce inbound phone calls and centralize inquiries that would otherwise be scattered across email, voicemail, and contact forms.

### "I don't want another platform"

> Web Chat feeds directly into the same Inbox where all your other communications live. There's no separate dashboard or tool to check — it's all in one place.

<div className="in-practice">
**In practice:** A busy HVAC company was skeptical about Web Chat because the owner felt like he was already at capacity. After enabling it, he noticed fewer phone tag situations — customers left their info through Web Chat and his team texted them back during slower periods instead of playing phone tag all day.
</div>
