When you log in to the app, you land directly in the Inbox — your central hub for every customer communication. From here, your entire team can see conversations, respond to leads, and manage contacts without switching between tools.

:::tip
The Inbox is designed to replace the chaos of checking multiple platforms. Once you understand how it's organized, managing customer communications becomes significantly faster and more consistent.
:::

## <div className="principle-area"><span className="principle-area__icon">🏠</span>Benefits of the Inbox</div>

### One place for all leads

> Every lead from Google, Facebook, your website, and Web Chat lands in the Inbox. No more checking separate platforms or missing inquiries that came in through a channel you forgot to check.

### Complete interaction history

> Every message, review request, and lead conversation is stored in the contact's thread. When a team member opens a conversation, they have full context — enabling personalized, informed responses.

### A shared team inbox

> Every team member with access can see and manage the same Inbox. This creates transparency and ensures that no conversation gets overlooked because one person was unavailable.

### Where you start every day

> The Inbox is the default landing page when you log in. It's designed to be the first thing you see — because it contains everything that needs your attention.

<div className="in-practice">
**In practice:** A spa owner logs in each morning and immediately sees three new leads — one from Google Messages, one from Web Chat, and one from Facebook Messenger. Her front desk team has already responded to two of them. She only needs to handle the third, and the full conversation history is right there so she knows exactly what was already said.
</div>

## <div className="principle-area"><span className="principle-area__icon">📨</span>Types of Communications</div>

### Messages

> Standard outbound messages are sent by text if the contact has a phone number on file. If only an email address is available, the message is sent by email instead. The app prioritizes texting over email by default.

### Review requests

> Review requests appear in the Inbox in a shortened format. The first request goes to Google. Follow-up requests use Smarter Ask logic to route to other review sites based on what will be most impactful.

### Leads

> Leads from Web Chat, Google Messages, Facebook Messenger, and website contact forms all appear in the Inbox. Each lead includes the contact's submitted information and the conversation thread.

### Appointment reminders

> For businesses using Shop-Ware or compatible integrations, appointment reminders are also managed and visible through the Inbox.

<div className="in-practice">
**In practice:** A mechanic shop using Shop-Ware sees appointment reminders fire automatically for scheduled services. In the same Inbox view, they can see new leads from Web Chat and follow up on a review request that went unanswered — all without switching tools.
</div>

## <div className="principle-area"><span className="principle-area__icon">💬</span>Communicating with Contacts</div>

### Accessing a conversation

> Click on any contact or lead in the Inbox to open their conversation thread. The full history of all past messages is visible immediately.

### Sending files and images

> From within a conversation, you can attach and send files or images directly to the contact — useful for sending estimates, before/after photos, or any document the customer needs.

### Sending a payment request

> You can initiate a payment request directly from the conversation thread, making it easy to collect payment without sending the customer to a separate link or platform.

### Sending a review request

> A review request can also be sent directly from the conversation — no need to navigate to a separate section of the app.

### Who can communicate

> All team member roles — Members, Managers, and Admins — can send messages and communicate with customers from the Inbox.

<div className="in-practice">
**In practice:** After completing a fence installation, a crew member opens the customer's conversation in the Inbox, sends a photo of the finished work, then fires off a payment request and a review request — all in under two minutes, right from their phone.
</div>

## <div className="principle-area"><span className="principle-area__icon">🔧</span>Managing Contacts</div>

### Editing contact information

> You can update a contact's name, phone number, email, and other details directly from their conversation in the Inbox.

### Disabling automated follow-ups

> If a contact is a repeat customer who has already left a review, you can disable automated follow-ups to prevent sending them repeat review requests. This is the right setting for loyal, long-term customers.

### Changing the contact method

> The default communication method is text. If a contact prefers email — or only has an email address on file — you can change their default contact method from text to email.

### Blocking contacts

> If a contact is sending spam or irrelevant messages, you can block them. Blocked contacts can no longer send messages to your Inbox.

### Deleting contacts

> You can permanently delete a contact from your Inbox. Note that deleting a contact also permanently deletes their full conversation history — this action cannot be undone.

<div className="in-practice">
**In practice:** A yoga studio notices that one of their most loyal customers — a client who has attended for three years and left multiple reviews — keeps receiving automated review request follow-ups. They open her contact, disable automated follow-ups, and the requests stop. She stays in the Inbox as an active contact for future campaigns without being over-messaged.
</div>
