## <div className="principle-area"><span className="principle-area__icon">📥</span>What is a Lead?</div>

### The definition

> A lead is an incoming inquiry from an existing or potential customer — someone who has reached out and wants a response.

### Why leads matter

> Before leads existed in the Inbox, businesses had to check multiple platforms separately to find and respond to inquiries. Missed inquiries meant missed revenue.

### What leads solve

> Centralizing leads eliminates platform-hopping, reduces response time, and gives your team full visibility into the customer journey from the very first message to the final transaction.

### One shared Inbox

> All lead channels flow into the same Inbox. Every team member can see every lead — creating transparency and shared accountability across your business.

<div className="in-practice">
**In practice:** A home cleaning business used to check Facebook, their website contact form, and voicemail separately every morning. After connecting their lead channels, every new inquiry appears in one Inbox overnight — and whoever gets in first thing can respond immediately without waiting for the owner.
</div>

## <div className="principle-area"><span className="principle-area__icon">🌐</span>Lead Sources</div>

### Web Chat

> Web Chat captures inquiries from visitors on your website. The app handles the initial response, collects contact info and consent, and pushes the conversation into your Inbox as a lead.

### Website contact form

> Your website's existing contact form can be connected so that form submissions arrive in your Inbox as leads rather than going only to an email inbox.

### Google Messages

> When customers message your business through Google Search or Google Maps, those messages come directly into your Inbox.

### Facebook Messenger

> Messages sent to your business's Facebook page through Messenger are routed into your Inbox alongside all other leads.

### Instagram Messaging

> Direct messages sent to your business's Instagram account are also routed into your Inbox — authenticated through Facebook.

### What is not supported

> The app does not receive leads from Yelp. Supported lead sources are limited to Web Chat, website contact forms, Google Messages, Facebook Messenger, and Instagram Messaging.

<div className="in-practice">
**In practice:** A restaurant connects their Google Messages and Facebook Messenger channels. Within the first week, they realize they had been missing dozens of Google Messages every month — messages they never knew existed because they weren't monitoring Google Maps separately.
</div>

## <div className="principle-area"><span className="principle-area__icon">🔌</span>Connecting Your Lead Channels</div>

### Web Chat setup

> Go to Settings > Web Chat. Copy the provided code snippet and paste it into your website's header or footer. The widget will appear immediately after installation.

### Website contact form setup

> Go to Settings > Web Chat, then scroll to the Contact Form section. Copy the unique consumer+xxxxxx@ email address and add it as the submission destination in your existing website contact form.

### Google Messages setup

> Go to Settings > Online Profiles > Google. Sign in to your Google My Business account to connect Google Messages to your Inbox.

### Facebook Messages setup

> Go to Settings > Online Profiles > Facebook. Sign in using a Facebook admin profile for your business page to connect Facebook Messenger.

### Instagram Messages setup

> Go to Settings > Online Profiles > Instagram. Instagram is authenticated through Facebook — you'll sign in with your Facebook credentials to connect Instagram Messaging.

<div className="in-practice">
**In practice:** A boutique fitness studio spends 20 minutes connecting all five lead channels during their onboarding call. From that point on, every new inquiry — whether it comes from their website, Google, or Instagram — shows up in one place for the front desk team to handle.
</div>

## <div className="principle-area"><span className="principle-area__icon">👥</span>Managing Leads as a Team</div>

### Who can respond

> All team members — including Members, Managers, and Admins — can see and respond to leads in the Inbox. There is no restriction on who can engage with an incoming lead.

### Why shared access matters

> When leads are visible to the whole team, no inquiry gets stuck waiting for one specific person. If the owner is unavailable, any team member can jump in and respond.

### Full interaction history

> Because all leads live in the Inbox, the full conversation history is available to any team member who picks up the thread. Responses stay consistent and informed.

### Managing the entire customer journey

> The Inbox isn't just for the first message. Once a lead becomes a contact, you can send them review requests, campaigns, payment requests, and follow-up texts — all from the same place.

<div className="in-practice">
**In practice:** A three-person auto repair shop has all team members set up in the app. When the owner is under a car and a Google Message comes in asking about pricing, the service advisor sees it in the shared Inbox and responds immediately — no lead lost, no phone call missed.
</div>
