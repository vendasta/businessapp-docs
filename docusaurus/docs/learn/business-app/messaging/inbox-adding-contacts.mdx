Before you can send review requests, campaigns, payment requests, or text messages, a contact must exist in your Inbox. This page covers every method available for adding contacts — so you can choose the approach that fits your workflow.

:::tip
The best contact strategy uses a mix of methods: automated integrations for ongoing additions, file uploads for existing customer lists, and manual entry or Kiosk Mode for one-off situations.
:::

## <div className="principle-area"><span className="principle-area__icon">🔄</span>Automated Ways to Add Contacts</div>

### Integrations

> Hundreds of integrations are available that automatically add contacts to your Inbox when a service is completed — no manual entry required.

### How integrations work

> When a job is marked complete in a connected software (like a field service or scheduling tool), the customer is automatically added to your Inbox and a review request can be triggered immediately.

### Leads as automatic contacts

> Contacts can also be added automatically when a lead comes in through Web Chat or Google Messaging. The visitor's info collected during the chat flow is pushed directly into your Inbox.

<div className="in-practice">
**In practice:** A plumbing company uses a job management integration. Every time a technician closes a work order, the customer is automatically added to the Inbox and a review request goes out — without the office team needing to do anything manually.
</div>

## <div className="principle-area"><span className="principle-area__icon">👤</span>Add Contacts Individually</div>

### How to add a single contact

> Navigate to Inbox > Add > Add Contact. Enter the contact's name, phone number, and email address, then save.

### Review request options

> After adding a contact, you can choose to send a review request immediately, set a delivery delay, or select "Don't send a review request" if the customer isn't ready.

### What you can do after adding

> Once a contact exists in your Inbox, you can send them campaigns, review requests, payment requests, and text messages.

### Assigning a team member

> If you are an Admin or Manager, you can assign an Assisting Team Member to a contact. This associates the team member with any reviews the contact leaves.

<div className="in-practice">
**In practice:** A salon owner adds a new walk-in customer manually after their appointment. She sets a 2-day delay on the review request so it arrives after the client has had time to appreciate the service — and assigns the stylist who did the work so the review is credited to them.
</div>

## <div className="principle-area"><span className="principle-area__icon">📁</span>Add Contacts from File</div>

### How to upload

> Go to Inbox > Add > Add from File. Select your file, then map the columns in your spreadsheet to the required fields in the app.

### Supported file formats

> Accepted formats are: .csv, .txt, .xls, .xlsx, and .tsv. PDF files are not supported for contact uploads.

### Required fields

> Each contact needs at minimum a name plus either a phone number or an email address. Both are recommended for full functionality.

### Review request options

> When uploading a file, you can choose to send review requests to all contacts in the list, or add them to your Inbox without sending any requests.

<div className="in-practice">
**In practice:** A new client has been in business for 10 years and has a spreadsheet of 400 past customers. They export it as a .csv, upload it through Add from File, and choose to send review requests to the full list — generating a wave of new Google reviews in the first week.
</div>

---

## <div className="principle-area"><span className="principle-area__icon">📲</span>Kiosk Mode</div>

### What Kiosk Mode is

> Kiosk Mode provides a customer-facing screen where clients can enter their own contact information directly — no staff involvement needed.

### Best use cases

> Kiosk Mode is ideal for physical locations like waiting rooms, front desks, service counters, or any place where customers are present while waiting. A tablet set up with Kiosk Mode lets customers opt in on their own.

### What happens after entry

> Once a customer submits their info through Kiosk Mode, they are added to your Inbox as a contact — ready to receive review requests, texts, or campaigns.

<div className="in-practice">
**In practice:** A tire shop places a tablet in their waiting room with Kiosk Mode open. While customers wait for their vehicle, they enter their info themselves. By the time the car is ready, they're already in the Inbox — and a review request goes out automatically after the appointment closes.
</div>
