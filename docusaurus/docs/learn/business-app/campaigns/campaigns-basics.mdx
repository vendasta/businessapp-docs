Campaigns lets businesses create and send targeted text and email messages to reach their customers — whether to promote a service, send a reminder, or re-engage someone who hasn't been in touch in a while. This lesson covers how to build a campaign, manage recipients, and stay compliant with TCPA requirements.

Campaigns is currently in beta, and active development has paused. Some details covered in this lesson may be subject to change. Check in with your team for the latest status before walking a business through setup.

## <div className="principle-area"><span className="principle-area__icon">📣</span>What Campaigns Do</div>

> **Core purpose** Campaigns lets businesses create custom text and email campaigns to engage and retain existing customers, re-engage past customers, and promote services to the right audience.

> **Audience targeting** Send to promoters, detractors, or leads using industry templates designed for common business scenarios.

> **Use cases** Promotional offers, service reminders, win-back messages for customers who haven't booked recently, and personalized follow-ups.

<div className="in-practice">

**In Practice:** Position Campaigns as a retention and re-engagement tool — not just marketing. Businesses often think of email as a "send blast" activity, but the real value is in reaching the right group (like detractors or lapsed customers) with a relevant message at the right time.

</div>

---

## <div className="principle-area"><span className="principle-area__icon">✉️</span>Creating &amp; Sending a Campaign</div>

> **Starting a campaign** Click "New Campaign" to build from scratch, or click "Templates" to start from a pre-built template. Both paths lead to the same editor.

> **Campaign type** Choose either an Email or Text campaign. This determines the editor experience and recipient limits.

> **Templates available** Promotions and Reminders templates are available for both email and text. Starting from a template or blank are both valid options.

> **Email Campaign Editor** Edit the subject line and body (the dotted blue area is the editable zone). Then select your recipients.

> **Text Campaign Editor** Same editing workflow as email. Note: there is a 320-character limit for text campaigns. The Send button is disabled until the message is within the limit.

> **What cannot be edited** The "Hello" greeting auto-personalizes to each recipient. The call to action ("call us" for email, "reply" for text), the signature (pulled from Admin Setup), and the banner image (edited in Admin Setup) are all locked.

> **Saving drafts** Click "Save" in the upper left at any time to save the campaign as a draft and return to it later.

<div className="in-practice">

**In Practice:** When showing the editor for the first time, immediately point out what is and isn't editable. Businesses often try to change the greeting or CTA and get frustrated. Knowing the locked fields upfront sets the right expectations.

</div>

---

## <div className="principle-area"><span className="principle-area__icon">👥</span>Managing Recipients</div>

> **Manual selection** Choose recipients individually from the business's contact list.

> **Suggested groups** Choose from pre-built segments: recent promoters, detractors, or leads from the last 3 months. These can be customized further after selection.

> **Recipient limits** Email campaigns support up to 1,500 recipients. Text campaigns support up to 500. These limits exist to avoid triggering spam filters.

> **"Select All" availability** The "Select All" option is only available if the total recipient count is within the applicable limit.

<div className="in-practice">

**In Practice:** Suggested groups are a great starting point for businesses that aren't sure who to target. Detractors are particularly valuable — a well-timed personal message to an unhappy customer can turn the relationship around before they leave a negative review.

</div>

---

## <div className="principle-area"><span className="principle-area__icon">📋</span>TCPA Compliance</div>

> **Three consent types** Conversational consent is implied (casual back-and-forth). Informational consent is express (appointment reminders, welcome texts). Promotional consent requires express *written* consent.

> **Text campaigns require express written consent** Because text campaigns are promotional in nature, businesses must have express written consent from all recipients before sending.

> **Ways to collect express written consent** An opt-in checkbox on a form, a keyword text reply, sign-up at the point of sale, or a text opt-in on the business website.

> **Web Chat** Web Chat automatically secures express written consent during the chat flow — no additional action needed from the business.

> **DNC list** If a business cannot confirm proper express written consent for certain contacts, those contacts must be added to a Do Not Contact (DNC) list.

<div className="in-practice">

**In Practice:** TCPA compliance is non-negotiable. If a business pushes back on the consent requirements, explain that the liability is on them — not the platform. Walk them through the easiest consent collection method for their workflow, whether that's a POS sign-up or a web form checkbox.

</div>
