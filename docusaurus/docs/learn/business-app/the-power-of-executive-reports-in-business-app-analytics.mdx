<div className="principle-area"><span className="principle-area__icon">📊</span>Google Search Console &amp; the Executive Report</div>

Google Search Console integration brings top-level SEO data into Business App so you can show proof of your work to clients without building custom reports.

### Google Search Console Integration

> Connect Google Search Console to the Executive Report to show the real-world impact of your marketing efforts—website content, blog, listings, reviews, social media—all resulting in measurable SEO improvements. This is a no-cost feature available to all partners.

### Google Search Console and the Virtual Doorway

> Google Search Console impacts the **SEO** pillar of the virtual doorway: "Do you show up where people are searching?" By connecting it, you can show clients that their visibility in search is improving—building trust and leading to retention.

### The Executive Report

> The Executive Report is an automatically generated proof-of-performance report emailed weekly or monthly to your clients. It shows changes over time from all connected data sources—including Google My Business, SEO, reviews, listings accuracy, website analytics, advertising, social media, and vendor apps. Partners who use the Executive Report with a connected data source see a **51% increase in client retention** over 24 months.

### Do You Need the Snapshot Report First?

> No—but running a Snapshot Report is a good way to get immediate data into the Executive Report. When a Snapshot is created, a 7-day trial of Reputation Management is activated and data (listings, reviews, social) starts populating the Executive Report immediately. Many partners use the Executive Report solo without connecting it to the Snapshot Report.

<div className="principle-area"><span className="principle-area__icon">📧</span>Set Up &amp; Customize</div>

Before sending the Executive Report, set up data sources and customize what clients see—so they get the most accurate, relevant picture of their performance.

### Connect Data Sources

> Connect Google Business Profile, Facebook, Google Analytics, and Google Search Console before sending the first report. Clients simply need their username and password to connect accounts to Business App.

### Set Email Defaults for New Users

> Sending clients an email notification about their Executive Report prompts them to visit Business App and engage with their performance data. Clients receive a personalized email identifying highlights and areas of opportunity.

### Edit Notification Settings

> Partner Center &gt; Accounts &gt; Manage Users &gt; Search User &gt; kebab icon &gt; Edit Notifications. Under "Business App," edit which Executive Report notifications that user receives. Use bulk update from the Manage Users page to adjust notifications for many users at once.

### Configure Layout Experience

> Partner Center &gt; Administration &gt; Customize Business App &gt; Executive Report. Choose the layout section order, whether recommendations appear, whether the Marketing Funnel shows, and the default time-range for the report.

### Customize Recommended CTAs

> Each section of the Executive Report has a spot for one recommendation banner. CTAs consist of a sentence explaining a feature and links to use the feature, see solutions in your store, or learn more. Customize these to guide clients toward relevant upgrades.

<div className="principle-area"><span className="principle-area__icon">🚀</span>DIY, DIWM &amp; DIFM Approaches</div>

Three engagement models help you introduce Business App and Executive Reports to clients based on how involved you plan to be in their account management.

### Do it Yourself (DIY)

> A product-led approach where clients discover value on their own. Set up Business App with your branding, define the new user experience, configure your Store, and send a campaign to prompt new users to explore the app and connect Google My Business and Facebook. Run ads or embed an Acquisition Widget on your website to drive self-sign-ups.

### Do it With Me (DIWM)

> A collaborative approach where you and the client manage the account together. Even in a DIWM model, it's vital that clients know how to navigate Business App so they are empowered to upgrade from freemium to paid and build habits around using it. Walk clients through Business App using a guided talk track tailored to their needs.

### Do it For Me (DIFM)

> A fully managed approach where you set up and operate Business App on the client's behalf. In this model, clients still need to understand Business App's value—especially proof-of-performance features like the Executive Report, Inbox, and Meeting Scheduler. Use a customized talk track to introduce Business App and the managed solutions you're providing.
