<div className="principle-area"><span className="principle-area__icon">⭐</span>How Reviews Work</div>

The reviews feature is designed to do the heavy lifting — automatically asking customers for reviews, keeping all responses in one place, and ensuring a steady flow of fresh, relevant feedback for the business.

### Ask for Reviews Automatically

> When a customer is added to the app, a review request is sent on their behalf automatically — no manual follow-up required. This default delay is set to 4 hours after the customer is added, giving the business just enough time to complete the service before the request goes out.

### Monitor Reviews in One Place

> Instead of checking Google, Facebook, Yelp, and other sites separately, all reviews are consolidated into a single view. This makes it easier to spot trends, respond promptly, and stay on top of what customers are saying.

### Consistent, Recent Reviews

> Automation ensures reviews come in regularly — not just in bursts when the team remembers to ask. Consistent, recent reviews carry more weight with both potential customers and search engines.

### Review Gating Is Not Allowed

> The app cannot block negative reviews from being posted online. This practice — known as **review gating** — violates Google's policy and can lead to a business's Google page being taken down or suspended. All customers receive review requests regardless of their expected sentiment.

<div className="principle-area"><span className="principle-area__icon">📅</span>Review Request Cadence</div>

The review request system follows a smart sequence designed to maximize response rates and avoid overwhelming customers with too many requests.

### Initial Request

> By default, the first review request is sent via **text** and the second via **email**. The initial request directs the customer to Google via the Google Review Widget — Google is the top priority given its importance for local search visibility.

### Smarter Ask Technology

> The follow-up review request uses **Smarter Ask** technology to determine which review sites the customer is most likely already logged into. This removes the sign-in barrier and makes it easier for customers to complete a review. Currently supported sites: Google, Yelp, Facebook, Nextdoor, and TripAdvisor.

### If the Customer Leaves a Positive Review

> Another review request is sent **3 days later**, thanking the customer and directing them to a different review site to spread the love across platforms.

### If the Customer Ignores the Initial Request

> A follow-up request is sent **5 days later** to give the customer a second chance to leave a review or provide feedback.

### If the Customer Leaves a Negative Review

> No additional review requests are sent. The automation stops to avoid pressuring a customer who is already dissatisfied.

### Disabling Automated Follow-Ups

> Businesses can prevent future review requests from going out to specific customers. This is managed through the Disabling Automated Follow-Ups setting.

<div className="principle-area"><span className="principle-area__icon">🔧</span>Google Review Widget</div>

The Google Review Widget is an exclusive integration with Google's review platform that provides a direct, faster path for customers to leave a Google review.

### What Makes It Exclusive

> Access to Google's review widget is considered private and is only granted to a handful of select businesses that have passed Google's approval process. Most competitors do not have this integration — it is a meaningful differentiator.

### Faster Form Load = Higher Review Rate

> When customers click the review request link, the Google review form loads significantly faster through this widget compared to the standard Google review flow. A faster form means less friction and a higher rate of completed reviews.

### Trackable Reviews

> Because the widget is a direct API integration, it can track which reviews were left as a direct result of a review request. This gives a clear picture of how the automation is driving real outcomes.

### Where It Fits

> The Google Review Widget is integrated into the default **Direct to Review** strategy. It is the destination for the initial review request sent to every new customer.

<div className="principle-area"><span className="principle-area__icon">🌊</span>Review Stream &amp; Settings</div>

Review Stream automatically pulls recent high-quality reviews onto a business's website, and all review-related settings are managed in one place.

### Review Stream

> The Review Stream automatically displays all recent **4 and 5-star reviews** from Google and Facebook directly on the business's website. It also shows selected keywords and owner responses. To install, the business sends a **code snippet** to their web team or site developer to add to the website.

### What Review Stream Shows

> Review Stream pulls the most recent positive reviews, selected keywords, and any owner responses — giving website visitors immediate social proof without having to navigate away to Google or Facebook.

### Settings for Reviews

> All messaging and review settings are found in **Settings &gt; Messaging**. This includes the review request delay, request method (text vs. email), and other cadence configurations.

### Best Practice

> Automation is a powerful tool, but the quality of reviews ultimately reflects the quality of the business and the customer experience it delivers. The best results come from businesses that genuinely care about their customers — the automation just makes sure those happy customers are asked at the right moment.
