The Locations Report is designed for businesses operating across more than one location. It provides a side-by-side view of performance data — leads, customer activity, review request response rates, and sentiment — so owners and managers can identify which locations are thriving and which may need attention.

:::tip
The Locations Report is most valuable for multi-location businesses. If a business has only one location, this report is not their primary use case — point them toward the Team Report instead.
:::

## <div className="principle-area"><span className="principle-area__icon">📍</span>What the Locations Report Tracks</div>

> **Access** Navigate to Reports > Locations from the left menu.

> **Columns** The report includes: Locations, Leads, Customers Added, Customers Asked (review requests sent), Response Rate, and Customer Sentiment (Promoters, Passives, Detractors).

> **NPS note** Same as the Team Report: NPS and Passives are only meaningful for businesses using the NPS review strategy. For Direct to Review, NPS is n/a and Passives are 0.

> **Real-time updates** Data in the Locations Report updates in real time, providing an always-current view of each location's activity.

<div className="in-practice">

**In Practice:** When introducing this report to a multi-location business owner, frame it as their at-a-glance dashboard for holding each location accountable. They shouldn't need to call each location manager to know how they're performing — this report shows it in seconds.

</div>

---

## <div className="principle-area"><span className="principle-area__icon">🔍</span>Reading the Report Data</div>

> **Clickable columns** The Customers Added and Customers Asked columns are clickable. Clicking either reveals a customer-level detail view showing: customer name, email, phone, date added, and review request status.

> **Review request statuses** Each contact shows one of three statuses: **Not Asked** (hover to see why — missing info, invalid info, or added too recently to ask again), **Asked** (review request sent, awaiting response), or **Responded** (for Direct to Review = review left; for Yes/No/NPS strategy = survey completed and routed online).

> **Date selector** Defaults to the last 1 month. Presets for past week and year available, plus a custom range back to the year 2000.

<div className="in-practice">

**In Practice:** The "Not Asked" hover detail is often an eye-opener for businesses. Many don't realize that missing or invalid contact info is blocking their review requests from going out. Showing them this during a check-in creates an immediate, actionable to-do: clean up their contact data.

</div>

---

## <div className="principle-area"><span className="principle-area__icon">📱</span>Mobile, Filter &amp; Export</div>

> **Mobile view** On mobile, the report displays as cards — one per location — instead of rows in a table.

> **Filters** The left filter selects brands or groups. The right filter narrows to specific locations within the selected brand. These filters work the same as in the Team Report.

> **Export** Data can be exported to CSV for use in external reports or stakeholder presentations.

<div className="in-practice">

**In Practice:** For business owners who travel between locations, the mobile card view makes it easy to do a quick check between meetings. Exporting to CSV is useful for any business that integrates location performance data into broader operational reports.

</div>

---

## <div className="principle-area"><span className="principle-area__icon">🙅</span>Handling Objections</div>

> **"I don't understand the data"** Same approach as the Team Report: show 1–2 metrics rather than the full table. Make it quick and relevant to what they care about most — usually leads or response rate.

> **"I already track this manually"** Ask what their manual process looks like, then show one thing the Locations Report does faster. Even saving 10 minutes a week builds the case.

> **"I don't have time to look at reports"** Lead with the mobile experience. A 30-second card-by-card check on their phone is a realistic habit to build. The report is accessible, not burdensome.

> **"I look but don't act on the data"** Connect one metric to one action. Example: if a location's response rate drops, that's the signal to check whether review requests are going out. Anchor the data to a decision.

<div className="in-practice">

**In Practice:** The objection-handling approach for the Locations Report mirrors the Team Report — keep it simple, start small, and make the first win fast. A business owner who checks one number and takes one action is more valuable than one who ignores a complex dashboard.

</div>
