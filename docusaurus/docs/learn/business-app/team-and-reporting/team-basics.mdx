The Team feature helps businesses manage who's involved in customer communications, track individual performance, and keep internal coordination running smoothly — all from the same interface they use with customers. This lesson covers adding team members, understanding permissions, using Team Chat, and linking team members to reviews.

:::tip
Only Admins can invite, edit, or remove other team members. If someone is having trouble with team management, check their role first.
:::

## <div className="principle-area"><span className="principle-area__icon">👥</span>Managing Team Members</div>

> **Why add team members** Team members are added to help manage customer communications, send review requests, and track performance data at the individual level.

> **Adding a team member** Navigate to Team > Team Management > Invite. Enter the team member's name, phone number, email address, and job title, then select their app role.

> **Editing a team member** Go to Team > Team Management, select the team member, click Edit in the upper right, update the fields, and click Save.

> **Removing a team member** From the same Edit screen, click "Remove from Team" to delete the team member from the account.

<div className="in-practice">

**In Practice:** When onboarding a new business, walk through adding at least one additional team member together — even if it's just the owner's assistant or a front-desk employee. Getting the team populated early increases platform adoption and makes the reporting data more meaningful from day one.

</div>

---

## <div className="principle-area"><span className="principle-area__icon">🔐</span>Team Permissions</div>

> **Members** Can message customers directly. Standard day-to-day users of the platform.

> **Managers** Can message customers and access most features, but cannot edit Messaging Settings (for example, they cannot change the SMS review request image).

> **Admins** Have full access to all features and settings, including inviting, editing, and removing other team members. Only Admins can manage team membership.

<div className="in-practice">

**In Practice:** The most common permission confusion is around Managers not being able to edit Messaging Settings. If a Manager reports they can't change a setting, this is expected behavior — an Admin needs to make that change. Set this expectation during onboarding.

</div>

## <div className="principle-area"><span className="principle-area__icon">💬</span>Team Chat</div>

> **Purpose** Team Chat allows the business's internal team to communicate with each other from the same interface they use to message customers — keeping everything in one place.

> **Access** Navigate to Team in the left menu, then send a message to the whole team or to a specific individual.

> **What you can do** Send group messages to the whole team, send private messages to individual members, share photos, receipts, and tips, and confirm who is actively managing a specific lead.

<div className="in-practice">

**In Practice:** Team Chat is especially valuable for service businesses with multiple technicians in the field. Instead of switching between apps for internal coordination and customer communication, everything lives in one place. Lead with that context when introducing the feature.

</div>

---

## <div className="principle-area"><span className="principle-area__icon">🏅</span>Associating Team Members with Reviews</div>

> **Why associate** Linking team members to customers enables tracking of individual performance — including review request response rates and average star ratings per team member.

> **Manual association** Invite all team members to the app, then assign an "Assisting Team Member" when adding a new contact.

> **Via integration** Some integrations automatically include technician information and associate it with the relevant customer — no manual assignment required.

> **Value created** Association data makes it easy to identify underperformers who need coaching, recognize top performers, and even run review-collection contests to motivate the team.

<div className="in-practice">

**In Practice:** Businesses that run review contests based on team member performance see significant increases in review request response rates. Even framing the data as a leaderboard — not a performance review — tends to drive engagement from the team.

</div>
