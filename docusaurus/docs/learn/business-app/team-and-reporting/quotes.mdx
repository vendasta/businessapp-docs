Quotes let businesses communicate the cost of goods, services, and labor before work begins. Clients can accept or decline, and accepted quotes convert directly to invoices—keeping everything in one streamlined flow.

:::tip What you'll learn
How to send a quote from a conversation or contact page, how clients accept or decline, and how to convert an accepted quote into an invoice.
:::

<div className="principle-area"><span className="principle-area__icon">📋</span>Sending Quotes</div>

Quotes can be initiated in two ways — directly from a customer conversation or from the contact details page.

### From the Conversation View

> Open a customer conversation and select the option to send a quote. This keeps the quote in context with the ongoing customer communication thread.

### From the Contact Details Page

> Navigate to a contact's details page and initiate a quote from there. Both methods produce the same quote for the customer.

### Requesting a Deposit

> When creating a quote, you have the option to check "Request Deposit" and enter a deposit amount. This lets customers know what is expected upfront before work begins.

### Viewing a Sent Quote

> After a quote is sent, it appears in the conversation activity thread. To see the customer-facing version of the quote, tap the "Sent" date hyperlink in the conversation.

<div className="principle-area"><span className="principle-area__icon">✅</span>Client Response</div>

Once a quote is sent, the client can accept or decline it directly from the quote link they receive.

### Accepting a Quote

> After the client taps "Accept," a confirmation modal appears. The conversation history updates to reflect that the quote was accepted, giving both parties a clear record.

### Declining a Quote

> If the client declines, the conversation history also updates accordingly. This gives the business immediate visibility into client decisions without needing to follow up manually.

### Real-Time Conversation Updates

> Any action the client takes — accepting or declining — is reflected in the conversation thread in real time. No need to check a separate system.

<div className="principle-area"><span className="principle-area__icon">🔄</span>Convert to Invoice</div>

Accepted quotes can be converted into invoices directly from the Quote Details view, saving time and reducing data entry errors.

### One-Click Conversion

> Open the accepted quote in Quote Details and select the convert-to-invoice option. The invoice is pre-populated with the same line items, prices, and notes from the quote.

### Edit Before Sending

> Before sending the invoice, you can edit the line items and notes if anything has changed since the quote was accepted. Once you're satisfied, send the invoice as normal.

### No Invoice Limit on Line Items

> There is no limit to how many line items you can add when creating a quote or invoice. Add as many goods, services, or labor entries as the job requires.
