The Team Report gives businesses a clear view of how each team member is performing when it comes to customer engagement and review generation. Whether a business wants to identify who needs coaching or celebrate top performers, this report makes that data visible and actionable.

:::tip
Team members must be associated with customers before their data appears in the Team Report. If a business sees missing data, check whether team members have been properly linked to contacts.
:::

## <div className="principle-area"><span className="principle-area__icon">📊</span>What the Team Report Tracks</div>

> **Access** Navigate to Reports > Team from the left menu.

> **Metrics tracked** For each team member, the report shows: Customers Added, Customers Asked (review requests sent), Response Rate, Customer Sentiment (Promoters, Passives, Detractors), NPS score, and a Review Generation Breakout by platform (Google, Facebook, Yelp, Nextdoor).

> **NPS note** NPS and Passives are only meaningful for businesses using the NPS review strategy. For businesses using Direct to Review, NPS shows as n/a and Passives show as 0.

<div className="in-practice">

**In Practice:** When walking a business through the Team Report for the first time, focus on Response Rate and Customer Sentiment before going into NPS or platform breakouts. Those two columns give an immediate, intuitive read on how each team member is performing.

</div>

---

## <div className="principle-area"><span className="principle-area__icon">🔍</span>Reading the Report</div>

> **Date selector** Defaults to the last 1 month. Preset options for the past week or year, plus a custom range that goes back to the year 2000.

> **Brand and location filters** The left filter selects brands or "Other locations." The right filter then narrows to specific locations within the selected group. A specific location must be selected to see team-level activity.

> **Columns explained** Each column represents a distinct performance dimension. Response Rate shows what percentage of customers who were asked actually responded to a review request.

<div className="in-practice">

**In Practice:** If a business has multiple locations, remind them to select a specific location from the right filter — otherwise they won't see team data. It's a small step that causes a lot of confusion when skipped.

</div>

---

## <div className="principle-area"><span className="principle-area__icon">📱</span>Mobile &amp; Export</div>

> **Mobile view** On mobile, the report displays as cards rather than rows — one card per team member. Cards are sorted alphabetically by team member name by default.

> **Exporting** Click the "Export" button in the upper right to download the full report as a spreadsheet. This is useful for sharing performance data with stakeholders or incorporating it into external reporting.

<div className="in-practice">

**In Practice:** The mobile view is particularly useful for managers who check in on team performance between appointments. Knowing the report is accessible — and fast to check — on a phone lowers the barrier for regular use.

</div>

---

## <div className="principle-area"><span className="principle-area__icon">🙅</span>Handling Objections</div>

> **"I don't understand the data"** Start with quick insights rather than a full walkthrough. Focus on 1–2 metrics that matter most to their goals. The report isn't about replacing their existing system — it's about adding visibility they don't currently have.

> **"I use manual methods already"** Same approach: find one metric the report shows faster than their manual process, and anchor the conversation there.

> **"I look at the data but don't know what to do with it"** Share specific examples — "when your response rate drops below 20%, it usually means review requests aren't going out on time." Make the data actionable with a concrete threshold or next step.

> **"I don't have time"** Emphasize the mobile app: the Team Report takes about 30 seconds to check on a phone. Building a quick weekly habit is easier than committing to a full reporting session.

<div className="in-practice">

**In Practice:** Objections to reporting are almost always about confidence, not capability. Businesses that feel overwhelmed by data just need a simpler entry point. One metric, one action, one habit — then build from there.

</div>
