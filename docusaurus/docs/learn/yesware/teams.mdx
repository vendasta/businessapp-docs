Yesware Teams let you share templates, campaigns, and content. Learn who can use vs create content, team limits, which subscriptions have access, and how to add users—including across payment accounts.

:::tip Access
Teams are available on Premium and Enterprise. Pro and Free Forever do not have access.
:::

---

<div className="principle-area"><span className="principle-area__icon">👤</span>Team Roles</div>

### Members

> Can **use** team content but **cannot create or edit** into a team folder. Team leaders and administrators can create and edit.

### Team Leaders

> Can create, edit, and manage team content. Can add users to the team.

### Administrators

> Full admin access. Can manage teams and users.

---

<div className="principle-area"><span className="principle-area__icon">📊</span>Team Limits</div>

### Number of Teams

> There is **no limit** on the number of teams an organization can have.

### Subscriptions with Access

> **Premium and Enterprise** have access to Teams. Pro and Free Forever do not.

---

<div className="principle-area"><span className="principle-area__icon">💳</span>Cross-Account Teams</div>

> Users on **different payment accounts** can be on the same team—**if both users are on Premium or higher**. Yesware support can add them. Users cannot add cross-account teammates without support.

---

<div className="principle-area"><span className="principle-area__icon">➕</span>Adding Users to a Team</div>

### Method 1

> Yesware > Teams > Select the team or create new > Click "Add a user to team" > Select the user's email

### Method 2

> Yesware > User management > Select user > Edit > Add user to team

### Why Add User Button Might Not Work

> If the "Add user to team" button doesn't work, the most likely reason is that **the user is not on the same payment account**. Cross-account adds require support.
