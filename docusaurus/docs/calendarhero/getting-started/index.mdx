CalendarHero is smart scheduling software for modern business. It helps you offload and automate everyday tasks like scheduling meetings, so you can focus on work that matters.

## Who uses CalendarHero?

**HR and recruiters** use CalendarHero to simplify the scheduling process and reduce time to hire. Scheduling across candidates, hiring managers, and colleagues becomes effortless.

**Sales teams** use CalendarHero to eliminate calendar tag and keep deals moving. Share a personal scheduling link so leads can self-book, from 1:1 calls to group demos.

**Customer success managers** use CalendarHero to connect with customers faster. From training sessions to implementation calls, scheduling is handled without the back-and-forth.

**Customer support teams** use CalendarHero to allow customers to book with any available team member using round robin and shared availability settings.

**Consultants** use CalendarHero to offload the entire scheduling process, so they can focus on bringing in clients and growing their business.

## How to get started

**Complete the Smart Set-Up.**
When you first sign up, CalendarHero walks you through a 3-step Smart Set-Up to review your meeting settings and get ready to schedule. All of these settings can also be updated later.

**CalendarHero works where you work.**
Get started using the [CalendarHero web application](https://app.calendarhero.com/meetings). You can also set up an automated meeting assistant in your preferred [chat or email platform](/calendarhero/setting-up-calendarhero/what-platforms-does-calendarhero-work-with).

**Customize your meeting settings.**
Review and customize your [meeting preferences and types](/calendarhero/how-to/how-to-customize-general-meeting-settings) for speedy scheduling.

**Schedule your first meeting.**
Learn [how to schedule a meeting](/calendarhero/scheduling-meetings/how-do-i-schedule-meetings) and get started right away.

**Manage your upcoming meetings.**
View and manage your active meetings from your [Tasks page](/calendarhero/scheduling-meetings/how-to-manage-your-meetings-from-tasks).

**Connect your apps.**
CalendarHero integrates with 70+ apps, including your [ATS](/calendarhero/what-can-calendarhero-do/how-do-i-integrate-my-ats-applicant-tracking-system), [CRM](/calendarhero/how-to/how-do-i-integrate-my-crm), [calendar provider](/calendarhero/how-to/how-do-i-add-and-customize-my-calendars), and video conferencing tools.

## What your assistant understands

When you [integrate CalendarHero with a chat platform](/calendarhero/setting-up-calendarhero/what-platforms-does-calendarhero-work-with) like Slack or Microsoft Teams, your meeting assistant understands natural language requests.

**Meeting types**

CalendarHero includes several pre-configured meeting types out of the box — like *coffee*, *call*, or *lunch* — and lets you configure each one to your personal needs. Learn how to [customize and automate your meeting types](/calendarhero/scheduling-meetings/create-and-customize-a-meeting-type).

**Contact names**

Your assistant learns who you talk to most and how often. CalendarHero uses contact disambiguation to figure out which "Mary" you mean, so you can simply say *"Schedule coffee with Mary next week"* once it learns your contacts.

**Dates**

Your assistant understands a variety of date formats — next two weeks, in 2 months, late next week, next Tuesday — and is constantly improving.

**Languages**

You can talk to your assistant in [almost every language](/calendarhero/what-can-calendarhero-do/what-languages-does-calendarhero-support), but it replies in English, Spanish, French, and Portuguese.

## Frequently asked questions

<details>
<summary>What is the Meeting Dashboard?</summary>

When you log in to the CalendarHero web app you are taken to the Meetings Dashboard. It gives you an overview of your favorite meeting types, with shortcuts to copy your [personal scheduling link](/calendarhero/scheduling-meetings/how-to-use-a-personal-scheduling-link) or [schedule a meeting](/calendarhero/scheduling-meetings/how-do-i-schedule-meetings) directly. You can pin up to 3 [meeting types](/calendarhero/scheduling-meetings/create-and-customize-a-meeting-type) to customize what appears here.

The dashboard also shows your workday: active meeting requests and reminders under *In the Works*, and upcoming meetings under *Today's Meetings*.

</details>

<details>
<summary>What is the primary booking link?</summary>

Every CalendarHero account includes a default primary booking link that cannot be deleted, as it is tied to your account. If you prefer not to use it, you can ignore it and pin other meeting types to the dashboard so those links appear first. All other meeting links you create can be deleted by clicking **Edit > Delete**.

</details>

<details>
<summary>How much does CalendarHero cost?</summary>

CalendarHero offers a free Basic plan with some limitations. The Professional plan includes unlimited meeting scheduling, custom meeting types, meeting and people insights, document generation, knowledge base, and 60+ integrations. The Team plan adds user management, corporate-wide settings, shared availability (including Round Robin), and room booking. Visit the [CalendarHero pricing page](https://calendarhero.com/pricing) for current plan details.

</details>

<details>
<summary>Why do I have to register with Google or Microsoft?</summary>

CalendarHero requires a Google or Microsoft email to access your native calendar, which it needs to check your availability and schedule meetings on your behalf. It does not replace your calendar — it enhances it with intelligent scheduling. After registering, you can [add additional calendars](/calendarhero/how-to/how-do-i-add-and-customize-my-calendars) and connect Apple and Salesforce calendars through [integrations](/calendarhero/setting-up-calendarhero/how-do-i-add-integrations).

If you plan to use a Team Plan, you must register with a work email address (name@yourcompany.com). [Learn more about setting up a Team Plan.](/calendarhero/how-to/how-to-set-up-a-team-plan)

</details>

<details>
<summary>Why does the assistant ask everyone on the Slack team to register?</summary>

CalendarHero works from each user's calendar and contacts. Every team member needs to register and authorize access to their own data before they can interact with the assistant.

</details>
