Your automated assistant is there to keep you up to date on all of your meetings!    
  
This includes automatically sending you:

- A morning briefing for a quick overview of your day

- A meeting briefing 1 hour before the meeting to ensure you are prepared

- Notifications for pending meeting responses (e.g. declines, meeting bookings)

- Notifications for overlapping meetings

## Customizing Notifications

Your "Preferred" platform (chat or email) will receive notifications; including the morning briefing and upcoming meeting briefings. 

You can turn meeting briefings on / off and set your preferred time for the morning briefing and meeting briefings in your meeting settings: [https://app.calendarhero.com/settings/meeting](https://app.calendarhero.com/settings/meeting)


 

## Morning Briefings

Every morning your assistant can send you a morning briefing of your upcoming day. This will include:

- the weather

- a list of upcoming meetings 

- the status of any pending meetings (including who has responded and who hasn't)

- an overview of your productivity savings

 


 


## Upcoming Meeting Briefings

Your assistant can send you a meeting briefing 1 hour before each meeting to help you prepare you for your upcoming meeting.    
  
Recruiters: If you [integrate your ATS (Applicant Tracking System)](/calendarhero/what-can-calendarhero-do/how-do-i-integrate-my-ats-applicant-tracking-system)then a link directly to the candidate's record in the ATS is now included in CalendarHero’s meeting briefings - making it easy for recruiters or hiring managers to access relevant information before they meet the candidate. A user simply clicks the link and is taken to the candidate record in the ATS system. 

Sales Teams: If you [integrate your CRM](/calendarhero/how-to/how-do-i-integrate-my-crm)then a link directly to the deal record in the CRM is now included in CalendarHero’s meeting briefings - making it easy for sales reps and account managers to access relevant information before they meet their prospect or client. A user simply clicks the link and is taken to the associated record in the integrated CRM.
