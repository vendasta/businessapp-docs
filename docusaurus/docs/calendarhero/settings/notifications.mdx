title: Notifications
description: Configure where and how CalendarHero sends you meeting briefings, reminders, and scheduling updates.

CalendarHero keeps you informed through your preferred platform — whether that's email, Slack, or Microsoft Teams. You can control which platform receives notifications, when briefings are sent, and what they include.

## Notification platform

Your default notification platform is where CalendarHero sends scheduling updates, morning briefings, and upcoming meeting alerts. To set it:

1. Go to [Installed Integrations](https://app.calendarhero.com/settings/accounts/list)
2. Click the **Chat** tab
3. Click **…** next to your preferred platform and select **Send Notifications Here**

If you use the Outlook or Chrome plugin, notifications always default to email — plugins cannot receive chat notifications.

## Morning briefings

Every morning your assistant can send a briefing with:

- Today's weather (based on your work location)
- A list of your upcoming meetings
- Status of any pending meeting requests (who has responded, who hasn't)
- A summary of your productivity savings

Set your preferred briefing time in [General Meeting Settings](https://app.calendarhero.com/settings/meeting/general). Morning briefings are sent via your default notification platform.

## Meeting briefings

One hour before each meeting, your assistant sends a meeting briefing to help you prepare. This includes the meeting details and, if you've connected a CRM or ATS, a direct link to the relevant record (deal, contact, or candidate) in that system.

Turn meeting briefings on or off in [Meeting Settings](https://app.calendarhero.com/settings/meeting).

## Reminders

You can ask your assistant in chat to remind you about anything at a specific time — for example:

- *"Remind me to call Roy at 11 am on Tuesday"*
- *"Remind me to log my time every day at 5:30 pm"*
- *"Remind me at 2 pm to prepare the NDA for the Monaco deal"*

Reminders are added to your calendar and sent via your default notification platform at the specified time. Recurring reminders are supported. To cancel a reminder, go to [Active Tasks](https://app.calendarhero.com/tasks/active) and click **Cancel**.

## Email footers

Add a custom footer to the automated meeting invite emails your assistant sends on your behalf. Go to [My Profile → My Assistant](https://app.calendarhero.com/settings/user) and enter your custom text. Your footer is added after the default email content.

Team admins can set a footer for the whole organization from [Admin Settings → Email](https://app.calendarhero.com/org/settings). An admin-set footer overrides individual user footers.

## Frequently asked questions

<details>
<summary>Can I turn off morning briefings?</summary>

Yes. Go to [General Meeting Settings](https://app.calendarhero.com/settings/meeting/general) and turn off the Daily Briefing toggle, or remove the briefing time.

</details>

<details>
<summary>What's the difference between a morning briefing and a meeting briefing?</summary>

A morning briefing is sent once a day at a time you choose and gives an overview of everything coming up. A meeting briefing is sent 1 hour before each individual meeting with the specific details for that meeting.

</details>

<details>
<summary>Can I receive notifications via email if I have Slack connected?</summary>

Yes. Your default notification platform determines where notifications go, but you can change it at any time from your Installed Integrations. If you remove or disconnect a chat platform, notifications fall back to email.

</details>
