Once you've created a template, you can insert it into any email in Gmail or Outlook. Templates support tracked links, attachments, HTML formatting, images, and merge fields for personalization.

## Insert a template in Gmail

1. Open a new compose window and add the recipient's address to the **To** field.
2. Click the **Templates** button at the bottom of the compose window.
3. Browse **Recent**, **Favorites**, or your full library.
4. Hover over the template and choose:
   - **Fill In** — opens the template to complete any merge fields before inserting.
   - **Insert** — inserts as-is. Use this only for templates without merge fields.

See [Use in Gmail](using-yesware-templates-in-gmail.mdx) for the full walkthrough including template reporting.

## Favorite templates

Click the **star** next to any template name to add it to your Favorites. In Gmail, Favorites appear under the **Favorites** tab in the Templates modal. In Outlook, they appear in the left-hand menu under **My Favorites**.

## Response Insights

Response Insights gives you data-backed suggestions to improve the predicted reply rate of your templates.

1. In the template editor, click **Response Insights** in the lower right.
2. View your template grade and the areas that could be improved.
3. Click each category for detail on what makes an effective email.

## Common questions

<details>
<summary>Can templates use HTML and images?</summary>

Yes. In Gmail, you can use the compose window to format your template visually and save it, or click the **&lt;&gt;** HTML mode button in the template editor to paste raw HTML. To insert an image, click the **insert/edit image** icon, browse your computer or enter a URL, and save. Outlook follows the same process from the **Open Templates** panel.

</details>

<details>
<summary>How do I add tracked links to a template?</summary>

Click the **chain link icon** in the template editor, enter the URL, and click **OK**. Links in templates are automatically tracked. Use descriptive anchor text (e.g., *View pricing*) rather than raw URLs to reduce spam filter risk. To disable link tracking, go to **Yesware Preferences** and toggle off link tracking for templates.

</details>

<details>
<summary>How do I add attachments to a template?</summary>

Click **Insert Attachment** in the template editor and select the file. For PDF and PPT files, you also get two options: **Allow recipient to download** (recipients can save a copy, but you lose visibility after download) and **Ask the recipient to enter their name to access** (gates the file behind a name field, and your Presentation Report shows who viewed it and for how long). See [Attachments](how-do-i-add-attachments-to-templates.mdx) for full details.

</details>

<details>
<summary>Can I use dynamic merge fields in a template?</summary>

Yes. Templates support Text Fields (free-form), Dropdown Fields (pick-list), and Salesforce Fields (Enterprise only, auto-fills from Contact or Lead). See [Merge Fields](../creating-templates/how-to-use-placeholders-merge-fields-in.mdx) for setup instructions.

</details>

<details>
<summary>Why is there unexpected spacing in my template?</summary>

This usually happens when content is pasted from Microsoft Word, a website, or an old email — hidden formatting carries over. Paste your content into a plain-text editor first to strip the formatting, then copy it into the template editor.

</details>
