# Can Templates use HTML and images?

Yes! Yesware Templates support **HTML formatting** and **image insertion**, allowing you to create visually rich and highly customized communications. Whether you're building directly in the editor or pasting your own HTML code, you have flexible options across both Gmail and Outlook.
### For Gmail
You can create HTML-rich templates in Gmail using two methods:
**1. From the Compose Window**
Open a **compose window** in Gmail.
Design your message with the desired formatting and images.
Click the **"Templates"** button at the bottom.
Choose **"Save as Template"** from the menu.
This saves your content to the Template library for future use.
**2. Direct HTML Input**
Click the **"Templates"** button and select **“New Template.”**
In the editor, click the **"&lt; &gt;" HTML mode** button on the right of the formatting toolbar.
Paste or write your **HTML code** directly.
**To Insert Images:**
While editing a Template, click the **“insert/edit image”** icon.
Choose from:
Browsing your computer for an image file
Drag and drop
Entering an image URL (source code)
Click **“Okay”**, then save your Template.
### For Outlook
Click **“Open Templates”** in the **Outlook ribbon**.
In the Templates library, click **“New Template”** (upper right).
To write directly in HTML:
Click the **"&lt; &gt;"** button in the formatting toolbar to access **HTML mode**.
Enter or paste your HTML code.
**To Insert Images:**
Click the **“insert/edit image”** icon in the formatting tools.
Choose from:
Browsing your device
Drag and drop
Adding an image via **URL**
Click **“Okay”**, finalize your message, and **save** the Template.
This functionality enables you to build branded, responsive, and image-rich email templates to elevate your communication with no coding expertise required.
