# Link Tracking

Link tracking lets you see when a recipient clicks a hyperlink in your email — including which link they clicked, which email it was in, and what device they used. Use this to prioritize follow-ups based on engagement.

When a tracked link is clicked, Yesware's server is pinged with the IP address and you receive a click notification.

:::tip
Name your links rather than leaving them as raw URLs (e.g., use "our website" instead of "www.yesware.com"). This reduces the chance of your message being flagged as spam.
:::

## Enable Link Tracking in Gmail

1. Click the **Yesware dropdown** in the top left corner of Gmail
2. Select **Preferences**
3. Go to the **Tracking** section
4. Toggle on **Track Links**

Once enabled, any hyperlink you insert is automatically converted to a trackable link. You can verify a link is tracked by hovering over it — if the destination includes `https://t.yesware.com/tl...`, it's being tracked.

## Insert a Tracked Link in Outlook

1. Open a compose window and click **Open Sidebar** in your ribbon
2. Select the **Insert Tracked Link** tab
3. Fill in the fields:
   - **Text to Display** — the word or phrase to hyperlink
   - **URL** — the destination link
   - **Title** — optional text shown when the recipient hovers over the link

:::tip
Links added to Templates are automatically set up as tracked links.
:::

## Frequently Asked Questions

<details>
<summary>Why do my links look like t.yesware.com?</summary>

Yesware rewrites your URL to a tracking domain (`t.yesware.com`) to capture the click event before redirecting the recipient to your original destination. The recipient lands on your intended page — Yesware just records the click in between.

</details>

<details>
<summary>Can I track links in a plain text email?</summary>

Yes, but HTML format is recommended. In plain text, the full tracking URL (`http://t.yesware.com/...`) is visible to the recipient, which can look suspicious.

</details>

<details>
<summary>Does link tracking affect email deliverability?</summary>

Occasionally, aggressive spam filters may flag tracking links. Using descriptive anchor text (e.g., "Click here" or "Visit our site") instead of raw URLs helps improve deliverability.

</details>

<details>
<summary>Can I search for emails in the Tracking Feed in Outlook?</summary>

The Activity Feed does not currently have a search option. To find activity for a specific email, use Outlook's search bar or your Sent folder to locate the thread, then click on it and select the **Message** tab in the Yesware Sidebar to view its tracking history. You can also search the [Tracking Report](https://app.yesware.com/reports/tracking) directly.

</details>

<details>
<summary>Why isn't link tracking working?</summary>

Verify a link is tracked by hovering over it — if the destination includes `https://t.yesware.com/tl...`, it's being tracked. If not, link tracking may not be enabled.

**Gmail:** Go to **Yesware > Preferences > Tracking** and toggle on **Track Links**.

**Outlook:** Use the **Insert Tracked Link** tab in the Yesware Sidebar to insert tracked links manually.

If you're still having problems after enabling link tracking, contact [support@yesware.com](mailto:support@yesware.com).

</details>
