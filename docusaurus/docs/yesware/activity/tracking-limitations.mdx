# Tracking Limitations

## Frequently Asked Questions

<details>
<summary>Can recipients tell if their email is being tracked?</summary>

No. Yesware uses an invisible tracking pixel to monitor opens and engagement. Unlike traditional read receipts, the pixel is completely hidden — your recipient won't notice any difference in their email experience.

</details>

<details>
<summary>Does Yesware track email addresses in the CC field?</summary>

No. Yesware only tracks email addresses in the **To** field. CC'd addresses are not tracked.

</details>

<details>
<summary>Why does the Activity Feed show "Opened by Google image proxy (66.249.82.0)"?</summary>

This IP address belongs to a Google image proxy server. When a recipient opens your email, Google sometimes loads the tracking pixel through its own proxy server rather than from the recipient's IP. Yesware has no way to prevent this — whether images are loaded by the recipient's IP or Google's proxy is entirely controlled by Google.

</details>

<details>
<summary>Are emails sent to .mil or .gov domains tracked?</summary>

Not reliably. Military and government domains often use specialized security software that blocks tracking features. Tracking events may not be registered for emails sent to these addresses. If you need confirmation of receipt, follow up directly.

</details>
